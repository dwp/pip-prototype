1. Overview

You can get Personal Independence Payment (PIP) a week to help with your costs of living if you have long-term ill-health or a disability that makes it difficult for you to live independently.

PIP payments can be from £21.55 to £138.05 a week.

How much you get is based on medical evidence and an assessment on how your condition affects your daily life and mobility. Your assessment may involve a face to face meeting.

You can still get PIP while you're working and it doesn't affect other benefits you or your partner get.

If you already get Disability Living Allowance then you'll be told when to apply for PIP - don't apply until you're asked to.

If you already get PIP

You must report any changes in your circumstances including changes to:

  • your personal details, eg if you move home or change your bank account
  • your condition gets worse or improves
  • the medicines or treatment you get

If you're terminally ill

If you have a DS1500 form from your doctor, you can apply for PIP by calling 0800 679 8900. You won't have to fill in the full application and won't need to go to an assessment.
