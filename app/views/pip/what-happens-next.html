4. What happens next

You must send written evidence to support your application, such as care plans or health assessments.

You’ll be assessed to work out the level of help you get - you'll get a letter telling you when this will take place.

Assessments can be:

  • based on the evidence you send as part of your application - you won't need to meet anyone face to face
  • carried out at an assessment centre
  • carried out in your home

You'll get a decision letter within 3 weeks of your assessment.

You can appeal if your application is rejected. Details of how to appeal will be in your decision letter.
