1. Overview

You can get Personal Independence Payment (PIP) of £21.55 to £138.05 a week to help with your costs of living if:

  • you're aged 16 to 64
  • you have long-term (over 9 months) ill-health or a disability that affects your ability to live independently

If you already get Disability Living Allowance then you'll be told when to apply for PIP - don't apply until you're asked to.

You must report any changes in your circumstances if you already get PIP.

What you'll get

PIP is split into two parts (known as components) one for daily living costs and one for costs if you have problems with mobility. You can be awarded one or both components, depending on your particular needs.

Daily living component Weekly rate
Standard £54.45
Enhanced £81.30
Mobility component Weekly rate
Standard £21.55
Enhanced £56.75

If you're awarded enhanced mobility you can also get a reduction in car tax. Anyone who cares for you may be eligible for Carer's Allowance.
